**Immediate Relief (Do This Now)**

1. **Hydrate Aggressively**: Drink 500ml water immediately. If you have electrolytes or a pinch of salt, add it to the water. Dehydration is a top trigger after late work sessions.
2. **Visual Reset**: Close your eyes for at least 15 minutes in a dark room. No screens. This reduces sympathetic nervous system load and relieves eye strain tension.
3. **Cold Therapy**: Apply a cold pack or ice wrapped in a towel to the forehead or base of the neck for 10 minutes. This constricts blood vessels (vasoconstriction) which often helps with tension/migraine pain.
4. **Drop the Lighting**: Dim all overhead lights immediately. Blue light from screens suppresses melatonin and can worsen headache intensity at night.

**Quick Recovery Methods**

1. **Box Breathing**: Inhale 4s, Hold 4s, Exhale 4s, Hold 4s. Repeat for 2 minutes. This actively lowers cortisol and heart rate to transition from "work mode" to "rest mode".
2. **Gentle Neck Release**: Slowly roll shoulders back 10 times. Do not force neck rotation; just release upper trapezius tension.
3. **Sleep Priority**: If you feel the headache fading, go to sleep rather than trying to finish tasks tonight. Sleep is the most effective physiological recovery tool for headaches.

**Prevention Tips (For Future Weeks)**

1. **Caffeine Cutoff**: Set a hard stop at 2:00 PM. Your current schedule likely involves late work; caffeine after 4-5 PM ruins deep sleep quality, leading to withdrawal headaches the next morning.
2. **The 20-20-20 Rule**: Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds. Set a silent timer if needed. This prevents focal fatigue headaches.
3. **Ergonomics Check**: Ensure your monitor top edge is at eye level. Looking down strains neck muscles which pull on the skull base (tension headache).

**Warning Signs (When to See a Doctor)**

1. "Thunderclap" Headache: Sudden, severe pain that peaks within 60 seconds.
2. Neurological Symptoms: Slurred speech, vision loss in one eye, numbness on one side of the body, or confusion.
3. Fever/Stiff Neck: Indicates possible infection (meningitis).
4. Post-Trauma: Headache following a fall or hit to the head.
